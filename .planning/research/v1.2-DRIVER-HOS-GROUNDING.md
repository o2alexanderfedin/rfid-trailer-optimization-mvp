# v1.2 Driver Hours-of-Service — Codebase + Domain Grounding

> **Purpose.** This document grounds the v1.2 milestone — adding **DRIVER Hours-of-Service (HOS)
> modeling, fully enforced in both the simulation and the optimizer**, with the full FMCSA rule
> set (11h drive / 14h window / 30-min break / 10h reset / 70h-8day cap / 34h restart, plus
> sleeper-berth 7/3 & 8/2 splits) and **driver relay/swap at hubs**. Today the operational twin
> moves **bare trailers**: there is *no driver, tractor, or duty concept anywhere in the code*.
> v1.2 introduces drivers as a first-class, event-sourced, deterministic renewable resource.
>
> **This grounding is adversarially verified.** A multi-agent workflow mapped the codebase,
> researched FMCSA Part 395 and the academic VRP-with-HOS literature, then ran an adversarial
> verification pass against the live source. The verifiers **consistently found the synthesis's
> effort estimates UNDER-estimated** — most areas were revised up to *high* or *epic*. The honest
> read is that **fully-enforced HOS is an EPIC, realistically ~3–5 weeks** end-to-end, not the
> handful of "small/medium" tasks the optimistic synthesis implied. Treat every effort figure in
> the integration table below as the **verified, corrected** figure, not the synthesis's first guess.

## FMCSA Rule Set (full, as integer-minute clocks)

US FMCSA Hours-of-Service for **property-carrying** CMV drivers, **49 CFR Part 395**, expressed as
integer-minute clocks (the sim runs `1 tick = 1 minute`, so minutes are the natural unit). The
"effective may-drive-now" predicate is:

```
mayDriveNow =
      driveRemaining > 0                      // 11h clock not exhausted
  AND now < windowDeadline                    // inside the 14h wall-clock window
  AND drivingSinceBreak ≤ 480                 // 30-min break not yet due
  AND weeklyOnDuty < weeklyCap                // 70h/8-day rolling cap
```

| Rule | Clock (minutes) | One-line meaning | Citation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **11-hour driving limit** `395.3(a)(3)(i)` | `maxDriveMin = 660` | Max 11h of *driving* after 10 consecutive hours off duty; a cumulative counter that **decrements only while DRIVING**. The headline "remaining legal drive time" number. | [law.cornell §395.3](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/49/395.3) |
| **14-hour on-duty window** `395.3(a)(2)` | `dutyWindowMin = 840` | May not drive after the 14th consecutive hour since coming on duty. **ELAPSED wall-clock** — does **NOT** pause for breaks/loading/fueling. Model as an absolute deadline `comeOnDuty + 14h`, not a counter. | [law.cornell §395.3](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/49/395.3) |
| **30-minute driving break** `395.3(a)(3)(ii)` | `breakAfterDriveMin = 480`, `minBreakMin = 30` | Driving prohibited after 8 cumulative hours of *driving* without a ≥30-min non-driving interruption. The break resets the 8h driving counter but does **NOT** pause the 14h window. | [law.cornell §395.3](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/49/395.3) |
| **10-hour off-duty reset** `395.3(a)(1)` | `resetOffDutyMin = 600` | 10 consecutive hours off duty resets the 11h and 14h clocks. This is the **mandatory-rest / parked-truck-at-hub** state — the central demo moment. | [law.cornell §395.3](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/49/395.3) |
| **Weekly cap (70h/8-day)** `395.3(b)` | `weeklyCapMin = 4200` | Rolling 8-day sum of **ON-DUTY** time (driving + on-duty-not-driving). FMCSA offers 60h/7-day *or* 70h/8-day; **pick 70h/8-day** to avoid the carrier-type branch. | [law.cornell §395.3](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/49/395.3) |
| **34-hour restart** `395.3(c)` | `restartMin = 2040` | 34+ consecutive hours off duty zeroes the weekly counter. **Must ship if the weekly cap ships** — otherwise a depleted driver never returns and long demos permanently park drivers. | [law.cornell §395.3](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/49/395.3) |
| **Sleeper-berth split (7/3, 8/2)** `395.1(g)` | `7h+3h` or `8h+2h`, neither period < 2h, total ≥ 10h, ≥ one period ≥ 7h in berth | Splits the required 10h into two periods; **neither qualifying period counts against the 14h window**. This makes the 14h "window" **non-monotonic** — the single biggest source of HOS modeling complexity. | [law.cornell §395.1](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/49/395.1) |

**Deferred even under "full rules" pressure (YAGNI — they do not change whether the optimizer can
reason about driver availability):** the 2-hour adverse-driving extension `395.1(b)` (only relevant
if the sim injects disruption events), the 60h/7-day variant (carrier-type branch), and the
short-haul 150-air-mile logging exemption `395.1(e)(1)` (a paperwork/logging exemption, largely
inapplicable to long middle-mile legs). Note that the v1.2 mandate **does** include the
sleeper-berth splits; the research flags them as the highest-complexity item, so they should be
sequenced last and tested hardest (the non-monotonic window is the trap).

## Recommended HOS Engine Design

Implement **ONE pure, deterministic forward-labeling HOS engine** in `@mm/domain`, shared (DRY) by
both the simulation and the optimizer. This is the **"rest-as-time" pattern** from the literature:
walk a route accumulating per-driver clocks, and before traversing any driving leg, if completing
it would breach a clock, **insert the smallest legal rest as time** and reset the relevant clock(s).
It is `O(n)` per route, seed-free, and trivially testable. The sim calls it to advance driver state;
the optimizer calls the same function as a feasibility/insertion check — so a sim-first build leaves
a clean upgrade path to optimizer enforcement **with no rewrite**.

**Per-driver `HosClock` value-object (integer-minute fields):**

```
driveTodayMin        // 11h clock — decrements only while DRIVING
dutyWindowStartAt    // ISO; the 14h deadline = dutyWindowStartAt + 840min (ABSOLUTE, no pause)
sinceLastBreakMin    // 8h-break clock
weeklyOnDutyMin      // 70h/8-day rolling on-duty sum
comeOnDutyAt         // ISO; come-on-duty stamp
```

**Four duty states (panel state machine):**

```
DRIVING  →  ON_BREAK (30-min)  →  DRIVING  →  RESTING (10h or 34h)  →  OFF_DUTY
```

**`remainingLegalDriveMinutes` formula** (the headline number for the Hub Detail panel):

```
remainingLegalDriveMinutes =
  clampAtZero( min(
      maxDriveMin       − driveTodayMin,         // 660 − driven
      dutyWindowDeadline − now,                   // ABSOLUTE wall-clock deadline minus now
      breakAfterDriveMin − sinceLastBreakMin      // 480 − since-break
  ) )
"may drive now"  iff  remainingLegalDriveMinutes > 0  AND  weeklyOnDutyMin < weeklyCapMin
```

> **Correctness trap — the 14h window is ELAPSED wall-clock and must NOT pause for breaks.**
> Model it as an **absolute deadline** (`comeOnDuty + 14h`), *not* as a decrementing counter. A
> counter would silently pause during the 30-min break and during dock dwell, **overstating** legal
> drive time and producing illegal schedules that pass the check. This is the single most important
> modeling decision in the engine. (The sleeper-berth splits make this window non-monotonic — that
> is exactly why they are the hardest item and must be tested against hand-computed fixtures.)

**Reuse of v1.1:** the engine reuses the v1.1 log-normal timing model **only** for rest/break
*durations* drawn in the sim — the HOS clock arithmetic itself is pure integer math with no RNG.

## Determinism Keystone Risk

**This is the gating quality risk for every other change.** The project's core invariant — *same seed
+ same scenario ⇒ byte-identical event stream* — is enforced by VirtualClock-only time, isolated
seeded substreams, stable `(fireTick, insertionSeq)` queue ordering, pure reducers, and
golden-replay tests. HOS must not break it:

1. **HOS-OFF must stay byte-identical to the pre-v1.2 golden.** With HOS disabled the stream must be
   *exactly* the existing v1.1 golden — no salt draw, no event, no ordering shift. This is what
   protects the rest of the codebase from regression while v1.2 lands.
2. **HOS-ON needs a NEW golden.** A new golden-replay fixture must be generated and pinned for the
   HOS-enabled stream; the existing `determinism.unit.test.ts` baseline (`{seed:1234,
   durationTicks:6000}`) is re-derived for the HOS-on case.
3. **All HOS draws flow through the new `hosRng` substream in deterministic queue order.** The new
   salt must be a **distinct constant**, asserted non-colliding with `0x5f1da7c3` / `0x3ca71d5f` /
   `0x00007717`. Rest/break **durations are drawn at deterministic evaluation time** (when
   `arrival + unload` completes — *not* at the wall-clock moment the rest begins), so that two
   replays of the same seed draw the same values in the same order.

> Any HOS draw not routed through the new substream, or a rest duration drawn at rest-start instead
> of at deterministic evaluation time, **breaks byte-identical replay** and the golden tests that
> guard the whole project. Prove determinism with a spike — *N drivers + 1 trip, replayed twice,
> byte-compared* — **before** building enforcement on top.

## VRP-with-HOS Modeling Notes

From the academic research item (citations below):

- **Truck Driver Scheduling Problem (TDSP).** Given a *fixed* route (ordered stops, time windows,
  known driving durations), decide *when* to insert mandatory breaks/rests so no HOS rule is
  violated, minimizing total duration. **Goel & Kok proved the US-HOS TDSP is solvable in
  polynomial time (`O(n²)` for n stops).** This is the green light: HOS scheduling along a fixed
  hub-and-spoke leg sequence is **cheap and deterministic** — no MILP needed.
- **VRTDSP (joint routing + scheduling)** generalizes VRPTW but its exact methods are
  branch-and-price column-generation MILP (Goel & Irnich) — **explicitly out of scope** per the
  project's no-MILP constraint. Borrow only the **decomposition pattern**: keep routing (the
  existing VRP heuristic) separate from HOS scheduling (a forward-labeling clock pass over the
  chosen route), implemented as a plain TS function.
- **Heuristic feasibility-check approach (no MILP).** The industrial precedent — **C.H. Robinson**
  (arXiv 1912.13157) — enforces DOT HOS as **feasibility checks inside a heuristic VRP**, not as an
  exact solver. This is exactly the v1.2 enforcement model: the shared forward-labeling function is
  the feasibility check.
- **Driver-relay handling.** Drivers are a **renewable resource** — a finite pool, each carrying its
  own clock, assigned to a trip. **Increment 1:** single-driver trips with mandatory-rest-at-hub
  (the "parked truck = resting driver" demo moment, zero optimizer change). **Increment 2:**
  **relay/driver-swap at a hub** — reassign the trip to a fresh driver in that hub's pool with
  sufficient remaining drive time; the depleted driver enters `RESTING`. This is how Amazon Relay /
  hub-relay networks keep equipment moving and is the compelling "fresh-driver swap" moment.

## Risks & Open Questions

### Risks (from `synth.risks`)

1. **Determinism regression is the top risk.** Any HOS draw not routed through the new isolated
   substream, or a rest duration drawn at rest-start instead of at deterministic evaluation time,
   breaks byte-identical replay and the golden tests that guard the whole project (verified salts in
   `engine.ts`: `0x5f1da7c3` / `0x3ca71d5f` / `0x00007717` must not collide with the new HOS salt).
2. **The 14-hour window is elapsed wall-clock and must NOT pause for breaks/dwell** — modeling it as
   a counter instead of an absolute deadline is a subtle correctness bug that silently **overstates**
   legal drive time.
3. **Optimizer enforcement risks plan infeasibility** if HOS limits are tight relative to leg
   lengths; the multi-pass VRPTW↔min-cost-flow feedback for rest insertion is an anti-determinism
   hazard. The rest-as-time `serviceMin` fold (KISS) avoids new edge types, but the hard gate **must
   reuse the proven Phase-2 LIFO validation pattern** to stay deterministic.
4. **Per-trip driver assignment (the MVP choice) is less realistic** than a persistent pool/relay
   and produces high churn in the assignment table; the "fresh-driver swap at hub" moment is a
   larger follow-on, not the cheapest MVP.
5. **Adding events to the closed union touches `contract.assert.ts` and every exhaustive reducer
   switch** (`assertNeverEvent`); missing a case is a compile-time break — manageable but a
   coordinated change across domain + all projection reducers.
6. **Scope creep toward sleeper-berth splits or adverse-driving extensions would balloon HOS state**
   (non-monotonic 14h window). Sleeper-berth splits *are* in the v1.2 mandate — sequence them last,
   test them hardest, and hold the line against the adverse-driving extension / 60-7 variant /
   short-haul exemption (YAGNI).
7. **Long-running demos with the 70h/8-day cap but no 34h restart would permanently park drivers** —
   if the weekly cap ships, the restart must ship with it.

### Open Questions (from `synth.openQuestions`)

1. **Enforce-vs-display DEPTH** — display-only, sim-enforced+optimizer-aware, or fully
   optimizer-enforced. *(v1.2 decision: fully optimizer-enforced — selects the longest `phaseOptions`
   branch and pulls `OPT-HOS-01/02/03` in.)*
2. **Driver assignment model** — per-trip (lowest complexity, recommended MVP) vs a persistent driver
   pool with relay/driver-swap-at-hub (more realistic, larger scope; required for the "fresh-driver
   swap" demo moment). Which does the narrative require?
3. **Weekly cap** — include the 70h/8-day cap + 34h restart in v1.2, or defer? If included, the
   restart must ship too (Risk 7).
4. **How many drivers per hub/spoke, and what initial HOS clock state at sim start** (all fresh off a
   10h reset, or staggered)? Affects when the first mandatory-rest event appears and the golden
   contents.
5. **Should the hub-detail rows show remaining drive time as a number, a bucket** (like the existing
   volume/SLA buckets), or both — and should map hub coloring use the same bucket vocabulary?
6. **Do the load/unload phase events also drive the optimizer's `serviceMin`,** or are they
   display/status-only for trailer state in v1.2?

**Factual corrections to carry forward (verified against source in this session):**

- **There are 13 domain events, not 17.** The closed `DomainEvent` union in
  `packages/domain/src/events/domain-event.ts` has exactly 13 arms (HubRegistered, RouteRegistered,

  simulation engine has **no** driver-state or HOS function today. The claim "reuse the SAME

non-monotonic-window item.
